When a horse is exposed to a flu virus that has drifted, antibodies partially recognize them but won’t form a perfect fit with the flu virus. As a result, the horse will have some degree of clinical protection from the flu, but may not have as much as direct protections.1, 2
This is called CROSS PROTECTION.
Cross protection may provide some degree of clinical protection, however, those horses may still shed as much as an unvaccinated horse.1
